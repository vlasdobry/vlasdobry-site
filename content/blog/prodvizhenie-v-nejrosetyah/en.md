**TL;DR:** AI search optimization (GEO) is a set of techniques that make AI engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude recommend your business. It works through structured data on your website, not through advertising.

Ask ChatGPT: "Where can I get an SEO audit?" or "Best hotel in downtown LA?" It responds with specific names. Not links — recommendations. The question is: where does it get them, and how do you get on that list?

I work on this professionally and see clear patterns. Neural networks don't pick sources randomly. They have specific criteria.

## What Is AI Search Optimization

AI search optimization means making your website findable, understandable, and quotable by AI systems. In the industry, this is called [GEO](/en/blog/chto-takoe-geo-optimizaciya/) (Generative Engine Optimization).

Traditional SEO fights for positions in a list of 10 links. AI search optimization fights for a mention in a ready-made answer. Users don't scroll — they get a recommendation instantly.

This isn't hypothetical. According to Gartner, by 2026, 25% of search queries will be handled by AI systems. In some niches (hotels, healthcare, legal), that share is already higher.

## How Neural Networks Decide Who to Recommend

An AI search engine doesn't show every website on a topic. It picks 2-3 sources and builds an answer. Here's what drives that selection:

### Structured Data

Neural networks "understand" sites with Schema.org markup better. When a page has JSON-LD with `LocalBusiness`, `ProfessionalService`, or `FAQPage` types, AI reads the data directly instead of guessing what the text means.

In practice, websites with full Schema.org markup appear in AI responses 40-60% more often. More details in my [Schema.org for business](/en/blog/schema-org-dlya-biznesa/) guide.

### The llms.txt File

Think of it as a business card for neural networks. A plain text file at your domain root where you describe your company in a format designed for AI. Without it, neural networks scrape your entire site and may draw wrong conclusions.

### Direct Answers to Questions

Neural networks love FAQ sections. Not vague descriptions, but concrete Q&A pairs. "How much does an audit cost?" — "Starting at $1,500, depending on site size." These are the snippets AI picks up and quotes.

### Source Trust (E-E-A-T)

AI evaluates the author: do they have expertise, are they mentioned on other platforms, do they have contact information and real case studies. An anonymous site with no author loses to a site with a verifiable expert.

## How It Differs from Traditional SEO

| Factor | Traditional SEO | AI Search Optimization |
|--------|----------------|----------------------|
| Goal | Rank in search results | Get mentioned in AI answers |
| Format | Link in a list | Quote in generated text |
| Key factors | Backlinks, keywords, speed | Structured data, FAQ, llms.txt |
| Competition | 10 spots per page | 2-3 mentions per answer |
| Verification | Google Search Console | Manual query in ChatGPT/Perplexity |

Important: SEO and AI search optimization don't compete. They complement each other. Schema.org, clean structure, E-E-A-T — all of this helps in both search engines and AI systems.

## What to Do Right Now

AI search optimization comes down to 5 concrete steps. I broke each one down with code examples in the [step-by-step GEO guide](/en/blog/geo-optimizaciya-sajta-gajd/). Here's the summary:

1. **Create llms.txt** — a text file at your site root describing your company, services, and contact info
2. **Add Schema.org** — structured markup for AI (LocalBusiness, FAQPage, Article)
3. **Write FAQ** — questions people ask ChatGPT about your niche, with direct answers
4. **Strengthen E-E-A-T** — an "About" page with facts, case studies with numbers, contact info
5. **Allow AI bot access** — check that robots.txt doesn't block GPTBot and ClaudeBot

## Who Needs This Most

AI search optimization doesn't work equally well for every niche. Best results come from businesses people search for with questions:

- **Hotels and restaurants.** "Best hotel in Miami Beach?" "Where to eat in downtown Chicago?"
- **Medical practices.** "Where to get an MRI?" "Best dentist in Austin?"
- **Professional services.** "Where to get an SEO audit?" "Family lawyer near me?"
- **Spa and wellness.** "Best detox retreat?" "Top spa resort nearby?"

For e-commerce with thousands of products, the approach differs. Product feeds and aggregators matter more there.

## FAQ

**Is AI search optimization the same as advertising?**
No. It's website optimization so AI systems find and quote your information organically. Like SEO, but for neural networks.

**How long does AI search optimization take?**
Basic steps (llms.txt, robots.txt, FAQ) take 1-2 days. Full optimization with Schema.org and case studies takes 1-2 weeks. First results appear within 2-4 weeks.

**Can I do both SEO and AI search optimization?**
Not only can you — you should. Many GEO elements (structure, Schema.org, E-E-A-T) also improve SEO rankings. It's not competition, it's synergy.

# Performance Max vs Search: What Works Better for Local Business

When I took over Google Ads for an appliance repair service in the Bay Area, the numbers were brutal: $500 spent, 1 lead. CPA: $500.

The market CPA for appliance repair in the Bay Area is $50–90 per lead. The actual average CPA was $307 — about 3–6x above market. In the first week, the CPA hit $500 — 6–10x the benchmark.

Six weeks later, CPA dropped to $13.91. Here's what happened.

## The Starting Point: Search-Only with Three Structural Errors

The client is KoxFix Solutions — refrigerator, washer/dryer, oven/stove, and HVAC repair across 13 cities in the Bay Area. Before I got access, the campaigns were set up like this:

1. **Broad match without Smart Bidding.** 275 keywords, 88% with zero impressions. Budget was being spent on irrelevant queries.
2. **No negative keywords.** Ads showed for searches unrelated to the service.
3. **Quality Score of 1–3** on 59% of keywords. The landing pages were a SPA without SSR, and every page's canonical tag pointed to the homepage. Google had no idea where to send users.

The result: average CPA of $307, 2 conversions in 30 days, and Smart Bidding stuck in "learning" for an entire month.

## What We Changed: Structure, Not Budget

### Step 1. Search Campaign Restructure

One campaign with Broad match became six themed campaigns by appliance category. 55 keywords instead of 275, all Exact match (starting point; later expanded to 125 as we added brand-name campaigns). Every keyword pointed to its specific service page.

Negative keywords: free, DIY, how to, used, parts, manual, video, repair kit.

### Step 2. Launch Performance Max

We launched PMax for refrigerators alongside the Search campaign. Budget: $20/day. Set up the feed, asset group, and audience signals.

**PMax Day 1 results:**
- 2,625 impressions
- 28 clicks
- 2 conversions, $225 value
- $11.47 spent
- ROAS 19.6x (Google later revoked one conversion — real ROAS: 6.5x)

### Step 3. Expand PMax to Other Categories

After the refrigerator success, we launched PMax Luxury Refrigerator ($30/day) and PMax Washer/Dryer ($30/day).

## What the Numbers Show: PMax vs Search Week by Week

| Week | Impressions | Conversions | CPA |
|------|-------------|-------------|-----|
| W1 (Apr 13–19) | 277 | 1 | $270 |
| W2 (Apr 20–26) | 460 | 3 | $186 |
| W3 (Apr 27–May 3) | 5,311 | 7 | $31 |
| W4 (May 11–17) | — | 44 | $13.91 |

W3 was the tipping point. That's when PMax hit its stride — 5,311 impressions, 7 conversions, CPA dropping from $186 to $31. By W4, PMax was the main driver: 44 conversions at $13.91 CPA.

**PMax channel mix:** 78% Display, 19% Cross-Network, 2% Search. PMax found leads in places the Search campaign couldn't reach.

Meanwhile, Search quality improved too: Quality Score climbed from 2.43 to 3.72 (+53% over the month). Impression Share rose from 19.8% to 50.6%. Lost Rank dropped from 80.2% to 30.1% — meaning the main constraint shifted from quality to budget.

## When Search Still Wins

PMax didn't replace Search entirely. Three scenarios where Search remains essential:

1. **Brand-name queries.** Exact match on appliance brand names (Samsung, LG, Whirlpool) captures the highest-intent traffic. PMax is less effective here — it optimizes for conversions, not brand coverage.
2. **Niche services.** "Sub-Zero ice maker repair" — low search volume queries where PMax won't gather enough data to learn. Exact match on Search delivers accuracy.
3. **Geographic control.** PMax can serve ads outside your target radius. For a business serving 13 specific cities, Search guarantees geo-targeting precision.

## Takeaways for Local Service Businesses

1. **PMax + Search as a pair.** PMax finds leads through Display and Cross-Network. Search captures existing demand. One without the other loses coverage.
2. **Exact match beats Broad match for local markets.** A tight keyword list with a limited budget — Broad match sprays money. Start with 55 precise keywords, not 275 loose ones.
3. **Landing page quality directly impacts CPA.** A SPA without SSR, canonical pointing to the homepage — basic technical hygiene matters. Quality Score improved 53% after fixing these issues.
4. **Don't judge PMax by Day 1.** Google can revoke conversions within 48 hours. Day 1's real ROAS was 6.5x, not 19.6x. Watch the weekly trend, not the daily spike.
